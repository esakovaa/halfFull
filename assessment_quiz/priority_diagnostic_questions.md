# Priority diagnostic questions (non-lab)

These are the highest-value non-lab questions to add on top of the existing model features. Several are not present in the supplied model inputs and are therefore recommended as new questions.

| Question | Best for | Why it matters | Already in model set? | Sources |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Have you been having hot flashes or night sweats? | Menopause | High-yield menopausal vasomotor symptom that materially increases pretest probability in midlife women, especially with irregular periods. | No | https://medlineplus.gov/menopause.html |
| Have your periods become irregular, lighter, heavier, or stopped for 12 months? | Menopause | Best non-lab discriminator for menopausal transition and menopause timing. | Partly | https://medlineplus.gov/menopause.html<br>https://wwwn.cdc.gov/Nchs/Data/Nhanes/Public/2017/DataFiles/P_RHQ.htm |
| Do you feel unusually sensitive to cold, constipated, and gaining weight without trying? | Thyroid problems | Classic hypothyroid cluster; much stronger than generic poor-health questions. | No | https://medlineplus.gov/hypothyroidism.html |
| Do you get heat intolerance, palpitations, tremor, or trouble sleeping while losing weight? | Thyroid problems | Classic hyperthyroid pattern and useful counterpart to hypothyroid screening. | Partly | https://medlineplus.gov/hyperthyroidism.html |
| Have you noticed swelling in your legs, less urine than usual, or foamy urine? | Kidney problems | More kidney-specific than general fatigue; useful when combined with diabetes or hypertension history. | No | https://www.niddk.nih.gov/health-information/kidney-disease/kidney-failure/what-is-kidney-failure |
| Has anyone told you that you stop breathing, gasp, or choke in your sleep? | Sleep disorder | More specific for obstructive sleep apnea than snoring alone. | No | https://medlineplus.gov/sleepapnea.html |
| Are you excessively sleepy or struggling to stay awake during the day? | Sleep disorder | Separates clinically meaningful sleep-disordered breathing from simple short sleep. | Partly | https://medlineplus.gov/sleepapnea.html |
| Do you get short of breath on stairs, feel dizzy, or crave ice? | Anemia | Exertional dyspnea and dizziness are strong symptom signals; pica or ice craving improves specificity for iron deficiency. | Partly | https://www.nhlbi.nih.gov/health/anemia/symptoms |
| Do you have heavy menstrual bleeding or black/bloody stools? | Anemia | Directly targets likely blood-loss causes of anemia and helps route workup. | No | https://medlineplus.gov/anemia.html |
| Have you had yellowing of the eyes/skin, dark urine, pale stools, or easy bruising? | Liver / hepatic insufficiency, Hepatitis B & C | Much more informative for hepatic dysfunction than abdominal pain alone. | No | https://medlineplus.gov/liverdiseases.html<br>https://medlineplus.gov/hepatitisb.html<br>https://medlineplus.gov/hepatitisc.html |
| Has a doctor ever told you that you had gestational diabetes, or do you have darkened skin on your neck/armpits? | Prediabetes | Gestational diabetes and acanthosis nigricans are strong non-lab risk clues missed by generic lifestyle questions. | No | https://medlineplus.gov/prediabetes.html |
| Have you had ongoing fevers, morning stiffness, widespread pain, or persistent malaise for weeks? | Hidden inflammation | Still nonspecific, but better symptom capture than relying only on sleep and lifestyle proxies. | No | https://medlineplus.gov/lab-tests/c-reactive-protein-crp-test/ |
| Have you had vomiting, diarrhea, heavy sweating, muscle cramps, weakness, or palpitations recently? | Electrolyte deficiency / imbalance | Higher-yield for acute electrolyte problems than chronic disease history alone. | No | https://medlineplus.gov/fluidandelectrolytebalance.html |
| Have you ever had a blood transfusion, injected drugs, dialysis, or tattoos/piercings in non-sterile settings? | Hepatitis B & C | Risk-factor screening is more useful than symptoms because chronic HBV/HCV is often silent. | Partly | https://medlineplus.gov/hepatitisb.html<br>https://medlineplus.gov/hepatitisc.html |

## Ordering suggestion

1. Ask the high-specificity symptom questions first: night sweats/hot flashes, witnessed apneas, jaundice/dark urine, gestational diabetes history, transfusion / injection-drug / tattoo exposure.
2. Ask shared symptom clusters next: fatigue, shortness of breath on exertion, nocturia, muscle cramps, weight change, cold/heat intolerance.
3. Ask broad lifestyle questions after that: smoking, alcohol, activity, and work schedule.
